# Technical Element Spec

> This spec provides a structured framework for generating a rigorous report on a **technical element**.

## Conceptual Definition

A **technical element** is a fundamental unit of technology. Technology can be decomposed into two primary components:

* **Technical Objects**
* **Techniques**

### Technical Objects

Physical or digital artifacts, systems, or devices designed to perform specific functions. They **embody constitutive techniques**, serving as the material realization of technology and enabling its deployment in practical contexts.

### Techniques

Structured methods of action that mobilize knowledge, tools, and materials to transform **matter, energy, or information**.

## Typology of Technical Elements

1. **Technique**
   A general method or procedure for achieving a transformation.

2. **Constitutive Technique**
   A technique embedded within the structure or functioning of a technical object or process.
   *Examples: semiconductor doping, metallurgical alloying, fermentation.*

3. **Operative Technique**
   A technique governing the use, operation, and coordination of technical objects within a system.
   *Examples: CNC operation, assembly-line coordination.*

4. **Production Technical Object (Capital Good)**
   A technical object used directly in production processes rather than for final consumption.

5. **Constitutive Technical Object**
   A component that forms an integral functional part of a larger product.
   *Examples: microchip in a smartphone, battery in an EV, enzyme in a biopharmaceutical.*

## Analytical Dimensions

### 1. Formulation

* **Technique Characterization**
  What transformation does the technique perform?

* **Technique Formulation**
  Formal description (procedural steps, equations, or rules).

* **Technical Nature**
  Domain: physical, chemical, biological, informational, or hybrid.

* **Technique Embodiment**
  How the technique is embedded:

  * In artifacts (machines, materials)
  * In humans (skills, tacit knowledge)
  * In code (algorithms, software systems)

* **Value Potential**
  What value is generated by mastering or controlling this technique?

---

### 2. Origin

* What is the historical origin of the technique?
* Under what constraints or problems did it emerge?
* Which scientific or empirical principles enabled it?

### 3. Operationalization

* How is the technique executed in practice?
* What are the required inputs (materials, energy, information)?
* What is the degree of:

  * **Codifiability** (explicit vs tacit knowledge)
  * **Trainability** (ease of transferring to humans)
  * **Automatability** (machine execution potential)

### 4. Topology

* How is this technique integrated with other techniques?
* What upstream and downstream dependencies exist?
* What is its position within a broader technical system or production chain?

### 5. Product Instantiation

* In which products, systems, or tasks is this technique used?
* Is it:

  * Core (differentiating)
  * Enabling
  * Auxiliary

### 6. Evolution

* What are the dynamics of change in this technique?
* What are the key R&D trajectories?
* What performance frontiers are being pushed?
* What are potential substitutes or competing techniques?

### 7. Replicability & Diffusion

* How can the technique be replicated?

* What are the constraints to diffusion (capital, knowledge, infrastructure)?

* What is the **learning curve**?

* What are the prerequisites for mastery?

  * Scientific knowledge
  * Tooling
  * Organizational context

* What capabilities are unlocked by mastering this technique?
