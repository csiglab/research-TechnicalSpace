Noetic Ontology

Index of
Social Actor Type

In this project we attempt to represent the abstract space of social actors at the planetary scale. The objective is to identify the most relevant actors across multiple categories—including individuals, firms, states, NGOs, and other organized entities.

By constructing this index, we aim to establish comparative metrics and analytical frameworks that allow systematic analysis of actors, their capacities, and their roles within global socio-economic systems.

Project Goals

Modeling Templates

Create a modeling template for each actor type, specifying its structural components (state) and dynamical components (processes and behaviors).

Actor Representation

Represent individual actors by applying the template derived from their actor type in order to produce a structured model of the actor.

Section 03

Representation Models

Ontological Layer

Defines the fundamental categories and relationships that structure the actor space. Establishes the vocabulary for describing actor types and their interconnections.

  • Type hierarchies
  • Property definitions
  • Relation schemas

Operational Layer

Specifies the mechanisms and procedures for instantiating actors from templates. Enables dynamic modeling and simulation of actor behaviors.

  • Instance generation
  • State transitions
  • Interaction protocols

Analytical Layer

Provides tools and metrics for comparing and analyzing actors within the space. Supports research and decision-making workflows.

  • Comparative metrics
  • Pattern detection
  • Impact assessment

Temporal Layer

Tracks actor evolution and historical trajectories. Enables longitudinal studies of actor development and systemic changes over time.

  • Timeline modeling
  • Change detection
  • Projection analysis
